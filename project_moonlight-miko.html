Game Project

Moonlight Miko vs. The Astral Troublemakers

A short, collaborative game project made for fun and learning!

Moonlight Miko is a 2D shoot-'em-up game created in collaboration with my friend and talented developer Tyler McAllister. The game is still in development, and is currently the main project I am working on outside of my studies. The first prototype was released on September 22, 2025.

Inspired by the colorful aesthetics of old school anime and magical girl series, our goal is to give players an experience that combines fun and comforting worldbuilding with challenge and mastery. The gameplay is intentionally challenging, as we wanted to create a fun contrast between cheerful presentation and demanding gameplay.

The initial prototype features a single level, and can be played both in-browser and as a downloadable .exe file. Our aim is to release a more complete version featuring 3 levels by March 2026.

My Contributions

While I originally only contributed to the game as the artist, as I have learned how to program and solve problems on my own, I have recently taken up most of the responsibility relating to UI and UX. As of right now, the menus and UI flow is my main responsibility alongside asset creation.

As for this project, I have had the following responsibilities

  • Designed and implemented the menus in the game: Title screen, intro screen, pause menus, popups, and display messages.
  • Designed controller descriptions and icons.
  • Implemented all UI and gameplay sound effects through a centralized sound-manager
  • Animation, background and other assets

UI and HUDs

The UI was designed to be simple, readable, and consistent. The menus are still going through constant iterations to reduce visual noise and to ensure that information about controls and progression is always easy to find.

By recieving feedback through playtesting and experimentation, I wish to create menus that are both visually appealing and easy to navigate.

Art and Animation

Backgrounds

The game consists of many different backgrounds and sprites, all hand drawn in Procreate and Clip Studio Paint. The backgrounds are drawn to work as infinitely repeating parallax layers, giving the illusion of depth and endless forward movement.

Level 1: Forest - currently used as the playable level in the prototype v.1.0.0-alpha

Level 2: Beach - this will be implemented as a playable level in the future.

Level 3: City - currently used as a placeholder intro screen.


Player and Enemy animations

The sprites are exported as spritesheets to make the process of adding frames faster, and maintaining a clean structure to the project.

A single animation typically consists of between 4 and 20 frames. As of right now there are about 15 unique enemy animations and several player animations complete. We consider the amount of assets finished to be about 50-60% complete.

Animation is hard work, and important animations go through many iterations to make them feel just right.

Going Forward

The plan for the project is to keep working on it and finish 3 complete levels by March 2026. This includes

  • Updating and polishing UI, and adding accessibility features and user configuration.
  • Adding cutscenes with unique artwork and dialogue.
  • New level and enemy design for Level 2 and 3, both in terms of gameplay and assets
  • Playtesting difficulty curves and collecting user feedback.
  • Creating a Tutorial level.
